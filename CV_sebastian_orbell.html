Thesis in progress under the supervision of Prof. Andrew Briggs and Dr. Natalia Ares. The general aim of my
research is to probe and advance the ecacy of semiconductor qubits for large scale universal quantum
computing. This is with a specic focus upon developing algorithms which introduce novel machine learning and
Bayesian techniques in the domain of optimal automatic control in semiconductor quantum computing
experiments. I conduct qubit experiments at ultra low temperatures using dilution refrigerators, write drivers and
code to interface with control electronics, and build autonomous systems to optimise qubit properties.
